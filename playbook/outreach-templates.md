# Outreach Templates

Three templates. One channel each. Always personalize the first sentence using `best_opening_line` from the lead spreadsheet — never lead with the template body.

Tracking links: the `/landlords` URL should always carry UTM params so the spreadsheet ties replies to channel.

- DM: `https://tenanttrack.xyz/landlords?utm_source=reddit&utm_medium=dm&utm_campaign=q2-outreach`
- Email: `https://tenanttrack.xyz/landlords?utm_source=email&utm_medium=cold&utm_campaign=q2-outreach`
- Forum reply: `https://tenanttrack.xyz/landlords?utm_source=biggerpockets&utm_medium=comment&utm_campaign=q2-outreach`

Adjust `utm_source` per channel (reddit / linkedin / facebook / biggerpockets / youtube). Keep `utm_campaign` stable so the funnel rolls up.

**Length target:** under 110 words. Anything longer gets skimmed.

**Do NOT:**
- Open with "Hope this finds you well" or any throat-clearing
- Use the word "leverage", "synergy", "platform", or "solution"
- Attach a 1-pager. The Loom IS the 1-pager.
- Send a calendar link in the first message. Wait for the reply.

## Template 3 — Forum / Reddit comment reply (be helpful FIRST)

This is the highest-converting channel because you're showing up where the pain is being expressed in real time. Rule: **answer their question or address their pain in the first 80% of the reply.** The product mention is the last sentence and is soft.

**Pattern:**

> [Direct, useful answer to what they asked / agreement with what they're frustrated about, with one specific tactic or insight that is genuinely valuable even if they never click anything else.]
>
> [One more sentence of useful context — maybe a stat, a story, a counter-perspective.]
>
> [Soft mention.] FWIW I built a tool for this exact problem (TenantTrack — QR codes for maintenance, vendor trust scores). Happy to send a 90-second demo if it'd help — or ignore the plug and just take the advice above. Either way, good luck.

**Example (in response to a "my plumber no-showed AGAIN" Reddit post):**

> The single biggest fix here is to stop dispatching by who picks up the phone first and start tracking who actually shows up. Even a paper notebook works — date, vendor, scheduled, did-they-show, did-they-finish. After 5–10 jobs you'll see the pattern and you'll have a real shortlist. The no-show plumbers self-eliminate.
>
> Also: send the request via text or email with the address, photos, and a window — not just a phone call. Phone calls give them plausible deniability ("I never confirmed!"). Written records change behavior.
>
> FWIW I built a tool for exactly this (TenantTrack — QR codes for tenants, trust scores for vendors). Happy to send a 90-second demo if useful, or just take the advice above. Either way, hope you find a plumber who actually shows up.

**Rules:**
- One comment-reply outreach per forum thread max. Don't dogpile.
- Never reply to your own previous comment to bump.
- If your comment gets downvoted hard, the message wasn't helpful enough — rewrite the helpful part, not the plug.

If both answers are good, you should absolutely stay. If not, the 90-second demo is here: [LOOM_LINK] — and I'll get out of your inbox either way.

(Always close gracefully. Today's "no" is next quarter's "wait, I changed my mind". And these people talk to each other.)
