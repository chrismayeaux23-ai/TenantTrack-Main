# Lead Sourcing Guide — How to Build the 200-Lead List

Goal: 200 qualified landlord leads in your tracking spreadsheet by end of Week 1. Quality > quantity. A bad lead wastes 10 minutes per outreach and pollutes your reply rate, which makes it harder to read what's working.

## Who counts as a qualified lead

A lead goes into the spreadsheet only if **all four** are true:

1. **Self-managing landlord** — they manage their own properties (not a property management company managing on someone else's behalf, not an institutional fund).
2. **10–50 units** — small enough to feel maintenance pain personally, big enough to pay $50–$150/mo without a procurement process.
3. **US-based** — for now. International is a different market.
4. **Recent pain signal** — within the last 90 days they have publicly posted, commented, or otherwise shown evidence of a maintenance-related pain point.

Examples of pain signals that qualify:

- A Reddit post asking how others handle after-hours maintenance calls
- A BiggerPockets thread complaining about a no-show contractor
- A LinkedIn post about a tenant lost over a slow repair
- A Facebook group comment asking for a vendor recommendation
- A YouTube comment under a property-management video describing maintenance chaos

If you cannot point to a specific URL with the pain signal, the lead is not ready. Park it in a "warm leads" tab and revisit when you find a signal.

## Where to source

Spend roughly this much time on each channel during Week 1:

| Channel | Time | Approx. leads | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| BiggerPockets forums | 90 min | 50 | Filter forums to "Managing Your Property" and "Tenant Screening". Sort by recent. |
| r/Landlord | 60 min | 35 | Sort posts by "New" and "Top this month". Read comments — commenters are often more qualified than OP. |
| r/realestateinvesting | 45 min | 25 | Filter for posts tagged Property Management. |
| LinkedIn search | 60 min | 40 | Search: `"landlord" OR "rental property owner"` + filter by Posts in last 30 days. Check profile for unit count clues (bio, recent posts). |
| Facebook groups | 60 min | 40 | Join 5–10 large landlord groups (search "landlord" + your state, and national groups like "Landlords Association"). Filter group posts by "Recent". |
| YouTube comments | 30 min | 10 | Comments under videos by Coach Carson, BiggerPockets channel, Lili Invests. People who comment with their portfolio size = gold. |

## How to estimate unit count

You will rarely see "I own 23 units" stated outright. Use these signals:

- **Bio / profile**: "Real estate investor managing 18 doors in Austin"
- **Post content**: "across my 12 properties..."
- **Comment history**: scroll their last 20 posts/comments — pattern emerges fast
- **LinkedIn**: title often says "Owner" + company name. Google the company name.

Acceptable estimates:

- "Definitely 10+" → qualifies
- "Probably 50+ but might be institutional" → check, often skip
- "Sounds like 1–3 units, brand new" → skip — too small to pay

When in doubt, skip. Marginal leads dilute your message.

## How to capture

Open `lead-list-template.csv` in Google Sheets. Every lead gets one row. Required columns:

- **Name** — first name if known, "Username (display name)" otherwise
- **Channel** — Reddit / BiggerPockets / LinkedIn / FB / YouTube / Email
- **Profile URL** — direct link to their profile or post
- **Contact handle / email** — what you'll actually message
- **Estimated units** — 10–25 / 26–50 / 50+
- **Pain signal URL** — direct link to the post/comment that qualified them
- **Pain signal in their words** — copy/paste 1–2 sentences from their post
- **Best opening line** — the one personalized sentence you will lead your outreach with (write it now while context is fresh)
- **Status** — leave blank; the tracking system fills this in

If you can't fill in "Pain signal in their words" and "Best opening line", the lead isn't ready. Move on.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Mass-scraping a directory and dumping 5,000 names. You'll never personalize them, replies will tank, and you'll spam-flag your accounts.
- ❌ Targeting property managers (PMCs) to "expand reach". Different buyer, different pain, different price point.
- ❌ Targeting brand-new landlords (1–3 units). They don't have the pain yet and they don't have the budget.
- ❌ Targeting institutional landlords (500+ units). Sales cycle is 6+ months.
- ❌ Buying a list. Quality is unknowable, signals are stale, and the leads have been hit by every other SaaS.

## Pace after Week 1

Once the initial 200 are in:

- Add 10–20 fresh leads per week (Friday is a good "lead refill" day)
- Re-qualify any "warm leads" that post new pain signals
- After every paid customer, mine their public profiles + LinkedIn followers for similar landlords — these are your highest-yielding new leads
