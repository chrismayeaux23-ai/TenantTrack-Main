# Daily 30-Minute Execution Checklist

Print this. Stick it next to your monitor. Do it every weekday for 13 weeks.

The whole playbook is about consistency, not heroics. 30 minutes a day for 90 days = 65 hours of focused customer acquisition. Done well, that produces 15–25 paying customers. Skip 3 days a week and you produce zero.

---

## 8:00 — 8:30 AM (or whatever your "first thing" slot is)

### ☐ Minute 0–5 — Inbox triage
- Open email
- Any reply from a lead → flag it, do NOT respond yet (saves context-switch cost)
- Any new trial signup → flag it for the welcome-email step below

### ☐ Minute 5–12 — Reply to every reply
- For each flagged reply, open the lead in the spreadsheet
- Check `pain_signal_quote` to remember context
- Use the matching reply-handling template from `outreach-templates.md`
- Personalize ONE sentence
- Send
- Update the spreadsheet: `reply_received` = Yes, status = Replied, add note

### ☐ Minute 12–17 — Send 5 fresh outreaches
- Filter `Leads` by status = `Not contacted`, sorted by estimated_units descending (bigger landlords first)
- Pick 5 leads
- For each: copy the matching template (DM/email/comment), paste their `best_opening_line` as the first sentence, send via the right channel
- Update each row: `first_contacted_date` = today, `channel_used` = channel, status = Contacted

### ☐ Minute 17–22 — Process the follow-up queue
- Filter for pale-yellow rows (4+ days since first contact, no reply)
- Send the ONE-line follow-up from the template
- Update status = "Followed up"
- Limit: 5 follow-ups per day. If queue is bigger, oldest first.

### ☐ Minute 22–28 — Trial pipeline maintenance
- Filter for pale-orange rows (active trials)
- For each, check what day they're on:
  - Day 1 (yesterday): send welcome email if not already sent, send Calendly nudge if no booking yet
  - Day 3: send the "QR on a fridge?" check-in
  - Day 7: send the "halfway" check-in or schedule the nudge call
  - Day 12: send the conversion ask
  - Day 14 (if no convert): send the exit interview
- Update notes column with what you sent

### ☐ Minute 28–30 — Log + close laptop
- Add today's tally to a daily log row at the top of `Weekly Summary`: outreaches, replies, trials, paid
- Close the sheet
- Done. Go to your real work.

## On Friday afternoons (add 30 minutes — total 60 for the day)

### ☐ Weekly review (after the daily 30 min above)
- Update the `Weekly Summary` row for the week
- Look at the channel-breakdown table — which channel won this week?
- Open `Customer Voice` — what came up 3+ times?
- Write ONE change for next week at the top of `Weekly Summary`
- (Optional) Add 10–20 fresh leads to keep the pipeline topped up

## Common failure modes and the fix

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| "I sent 50 outreaches and got 1 reply" | Generic message, no personalization in the first line | Re-read `outreach-templates.md` — the rule is the first sentence is ALWAYS from `best_opening_line`. Stop sending templates with no personalization. |
| "I'm getting replies but nobody starts a trial" | Loom is bad or landing page isn't matched to the message | Re-record the Loom. Check `/landlords` headline matches the pain signal you're targeting. |
| "Trials don't convert to paid" | Onboarding SOP isn't being run | Open `onboarding-sop.md`, follow it literally for the next 5 trials, conversion will jump 3–4x |
| "I keep skipping days" | 30 min feels too long | Cut it to 15 min — 3 outreaches, 3 replies, 3 follow-ups. Consistency > volume. |
| "Replies all say no" | Wrong ICP or wrong pitch | Check estimated_units — are you targeting <10 unit landlords? Wrong ICP. Are you leading with features? Wrong pitch. |

## What you do NOT do during this 30 minutes

- ❌ Build a new feature (do that in your other work blocks)
- ❌ Tweak the website copy (Friday only)
- ❌ Read about other founders' acquisition strategies (procrastination)
- ❌ Refresh the dashboard hoping for new signups (procrastination)
- ❌ Respond to social media notifications

The 30 minutes is sacred and singular. Outreaches, replies, follow-ups, trial pipeline. That's it.
